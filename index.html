About the Artist

Immanuel Obani, professionally known as Immanuel The Artist, is a Bradford-based visual artist working across acrylic, charcoal, and digital illustration. His practice explores Afrofuturism, diaspora identity, memory, and cultural healing, carrying West African visual heritage — adire indigo, Akan goldweight, mudcloth geometry — into contemporary portraiture.

His work moves between painted surface and digital layer, using each medium's own texture — the grain of charcoal, the flatness of digital colour, the weight of acrylic — as part of what the image says. His pieces have been reviewed and featured across Nigerian and UK arts press, and shown as part of the Bradford African Festival of Arts (BAFA).
