About

Studio Notes

Immanuel works across acrylic, charcoal, and digital illustration, building an Afrofuturist portraiture practice. The work moves between painted surface and digital layer, using each medium's own texture, the grain of charcoal, the flatness of digital colour, the weight of acrylic, as part of what the image says.

Immanuel holds an MSc in Big Data Science and Technology with Distinction from the University of Bradford. His interest in British art curation centres on diasporic histories and culturally grounded practice, with particular attention to how art strengthens cultural identity across communities.

Immanuel is affiliated with the Bradford African Festival of Arts (BAFA) and Afrikindness UK, and continues to show and develop new work from Bradford.
